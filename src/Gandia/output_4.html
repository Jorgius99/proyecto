Palau Ducal Borja (Gandia)

View on OCity map

The Palacio Ducal de los Borja de Gandia, declared an Asset of Cultural Interest since 1964, is one of the most representative civil buildings of the Valencian historical heritage. Located in the historic center, next to the Collegiate Church of Santa María and the City Hall, it has witnessed the evolution of the history of the capital of Safor since its birth. Its interior hosts a varied display of architectural styles, with vestiges of the original Gothic palace of the XIV and XV centuries, Renaissance contributions of the XVI, extensions and Baroque transformations typical of the XVII and XVIII centuries, and finally neo-Gothic reconstructions of the late XIX century and early XX. It is a sample of the splendor of Gandia in the years in which the Borja family's relations with the Vatican allowed the city's economic growth and development.
